Autumn running in London

The humid of summer is long gone, but the chilling winds of winter are yet to arrive. As the leaves begin to turn and temperatures stay mild, autmn is the best time for running in the British capital.

With its abundant parks and leafy riverside paths, London really is a runner's city. These fantastic autumn races allow you to combine sightseeing with your exercise regime; and best of all, there's a medal at the end. Out Park inn by Radisson Hotel and Conference Centre London Heathrow makes the ideal base for a funning weekend in London, with plenty of comforts recovery(including a jacuzzi) and frequent public transport links into the city centre.

River Thames Hailf Marathon

This scenic half marathon takes you 13.1 miles along the banks of the River Thames, on the road from Walton town centre to the Xcel Leisure Centre. Most of the route after that follows the river townpath, although there are a few miles of road work. Look out for Hampton Court Bridge around the halfway mark. The race route vi very flat, so if you don't like running up hills or want to set a new personal best, this is perfect for you. It also makes the route particularly suitable for less experienced racers, so it's a good choice for your first half marathon. Registration stays open until 16 October, and very finisher gets to walk home with a goodie bag and a bespoke medal

When: 18 October
Where: Walton-on-Thames
Entry Fee: $29 ($27 with a UKA competitions licence)
